## Platform-specific notes

- Dog Furiendly has a public contact page and account/dashboard routes. Use the public contact route first and include the exact listing URL.
- Traveloka exposes Help Center/customer support and accommodation partner routes. Use both if available, because the page is a public OTA-style accommodation page.
- ReadyToTrip appears connected to a hotel/booking metasearch ecosystem. If the site contact route does not respond, use the contact route for its hotel/partner support and cite the page's own third-party-data disclaimer.
- Cottages & Lodges appears to use Sykes property image/data URLs on the page. Ask Cottages & Lodges for page removal and separately ask Sykes to confirm the property/feed is no longer distributed.
- Visit Lancashire is an official tourism listing. Ask for update/removal through Visit Lancashire or Marketing Lancashire rather than treating it like a scraper.

## Escalation path

1. Send the removal/update request through the platform's contact form or support email.
2. If no response after 7 days, send a second request with "inaccurate personal/property data" in the subject.
3. If old private phone/address details remain, frame the request as UK GDPR rectification/erasure of inaccurate personal data.
4. Once pages are removed or corrected, use Google's "Remove outdated content" tool for snippets still showing old information.
5. Track replies and screenshots in a folder outside the live website before publishing.

## Notes

- Do not claim a listing is fraudulent unless you have evidence. Use "outdated", "unauthorised", "inaccurate" and "misleading".
- Ask for removal plus `noindex`/de-indexing where possible. Deleting a page may still leave snippets in Google for a while.
- For Cottages & Lodges, the images/data appear to come from a Sykes-style property feed. Removing only the visible page may not remove downstream affiliate duplicates if the source feed remains active.
